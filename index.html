Investment Options

  • ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)

    investment funds that track certain stock market indices - for example the DAX. ETFs make it possible to invest broadly and cost-effectively with just one investment.

    Learn More

  • BoonGrowth Venture Capital

    Venture capital (VC) investment involves providing funding to early-stage, high-potential companies in exchange for equity or ownership stakes. Venture capitalists seek out startups with significant growth prospects that may not yet qualify for traditional financing options

    Learn More

  • Trading & investment

    structured strategy outlining specific objectives, risk management techniques, asset allocation, and investment selection criteria aimed at achieving financial goals. It encompasses a systematic approach to trading and investing in financial markets, incorporating elements such as defining goals, assessing risk tolerance, asset allocation, investment selection, risk management, monitoring, and review.

    Learn More

  • Certificate of Deposit

    CDs are time-bound savings accounts where you deposit a fixed amount of money for a specific term, ranging from a few months to several years. In return, you receive a fixed interest rate, usually higher than regular savings accounts, but with limited access to funds until the CD matures

    Learn More

  • Education Savings Plans (529 Plans)

    529 plans are tax-advantaged savings accounts designed to help families save for future education expenses, such as college tuition, fees, and room and board. These plans offer various investment options and typically provide tax benefits on earnings when used for qualified education expenses

    Learn More

  • Emergency Fund

    An emergency fund is a savings account specifically designated to cover unexpected expenses such as medical emergencies, car repairs, or job loss. It typically contains enough money to cover three to six months' worth of living expenses..
